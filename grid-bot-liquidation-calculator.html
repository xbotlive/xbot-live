Why liquidation is different for grid bots

Grid bot liquidation moves as you trade

On a simple leveraged long position, liquidation price is fixed at entry. On a grid bot, it is not. Every order that fills changes your position size — and therefore changes your liquidation price.

For a long grid bot: as price falls, buy orders fill and your long position grows. This reduces your liquidation buffer. The danger zone is not static — it approaches you as price approaches the bottom of your range.

A simple formula won't give you the right answer because it can't model the fill sequence. You need to simulate the order book dynamically to see where liquidation actually lands at each point in the trade.

xBot recalculates liquidation price after every fill across all simulated price paths. The output tells you the probability that your configuration gets liquidated under your volatility assumptions — not just a single number that assumes nothing changes.

As price falls toward $85,000 and buys fill, the long position grows — pushing the liquidation price up toward the range floor. At 5× leverage, the buffer is tighter than it looks at entry.

What drives liquidation risk

Four factors that determine how close you are

Leverage

The single biggest lever. At 2× you can absorb a 50% move before liquidation. At 10× it's 10%. For a grid bot with a 20% range, 5× leverage leaves meaningful cushion — 10× does not. Lower leverage is almost always the right call for grid strategies.

⬆ Impact: Very high
Range width relative to volatility

A range that's too narrow relative to historical volatility will be breached frequently. When price exits the range in a trending market, your grid stops earning but your position keeps building. Set your range to at least 1.5–2× the expected 30-day price move.

⬆ Impact: High
Grid direction

A long grid accumulates long exposure as price falls — exactly when the market is moving against you. A neutral grid accumulates exposure on both sides simultaneously. Direction determines which market moves hurt you most and how quickly your liquidation buffer erodes.

⬆ Impact: High
Number of grid levels

More levels means smaller position increments per fill, which slows the rate at which your liquidation price moves. But more levels also means more capital deployed per fill and lower per-trade profit. There's a balance — the simulator lets you test different grid counts side by side.

⬆ Impact: Medium
